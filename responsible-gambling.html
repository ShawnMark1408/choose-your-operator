Warning Signs to Watch For

Problem gambling can develop gradually. These are some signs that gambling may be becoming a concern:

Spending more time or money gambling than you planned or can afford

Chasing losses — continuing to bet to try to win back money you've lost

Gambling interfering with work, relationships, or daily responsibilities

Borrowing money or selling possessions to fund gambling

Feeling restless, irritable, or anxious when you try to stop

Hiding the extent of your gambling from friends or family

Tips for Safer Play

  • 💰Set a budget before you start and treat it as the cost of entertainment — money you're comfortable losing.
  • ⏱️Set a time limit and stick to it. Use your operator's built-in session limit tools if they're available.
  • 🎯Only gamble on licensed UK operators. Licensed operators are required to provide responsible gambling tools including deposit limits, cooling-off periods, and self-exclusion.
  • 🚫Never chase losses. The odds don't change based on what you've lost — each bet is independent.
  • 🍺Don't gamble when you're drunk, stressed, or upset. Emotional states impair judgement and can lead to bets you'd never make otherwise.
  • 📊Keep a record. Many problem gamblers underestimate how much they're spending. Tracking your bets makes the real number visible.

Self-Exclusion

If you want to stop gambling, GamStop is the fastest and most effective tool available. Registering at gamstop.co.uk will block you from all UK-licensed gambling websites and apps for a minimum of six months, up to five years. It's free and takes minutes to set up.

Individual operators also offer self-exclusion tools directly in their accounts section. These apply only to that specific operator, which is why GamStop (which covers all licensed operators at once) is generally the more effective option.
